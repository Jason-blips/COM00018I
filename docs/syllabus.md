# Syllabus

## Welcome to SYS3

The module introductory lecture is in the replay content. Module descriptions and learning outcomes can be found on the [SYS3 module website](https://www.york.ac.uk/).

### What are we going to cover in this course?

- Computer architecture introduction
- Processor design and performance: processor design and performance evaluation methods
- Instruction pipeline: instruction pipeline concepts and pipeline hazards
- Pipeline scheduling: static and dynamic scheduling, speculative execution, superscalar processors
- Memory: virtual memory, page replacement, cache concepts, and optimization techniques

## Weekly plan

Please check your [timetable](https://www.york.ac.uk/students/studying/manage/timetable/) for module session dates and times.

For each week:

- 2 asynchronous lecture units per week (approximately 2 hours reading/study time per unit)
- 1 synchronous lecture review session per week
- 1 practical session per week

Lecture review sessions and practicals run in F2F mode. Lectures in this module are pre-recorded.

There are 3 exercises and 3 quizzes. These are formative and are not graded, but students are expected to participate as much as possible to consolidate learning.

### Lab sessions

In your timetable, there is a 2-hour weekly slot for lab sessions (check your group). You are expected to join these sessions and work on your own lab task solutions. We encourage discussion in small groups (2-4 students) to explore ideas and understanding of the problems.

Practicals are problem-based. You typically explore ideas related to each week's topic, then develop solutions to problems related to execution pipelines and program correctness/efficiency. Solutions are released in the weeks following the last practical related to each task set.

Practicals are formative activities for learning. Knowledge and principles gained during practicals are assessed in the final exam with the taught material.

### Notes

1. Lecture videos are recorded as one or two short videos (around 25 minutes each) so you can manage your learning pace. You may find several short videos for the same lecture unit.
2. Lecture video naming typically follows:
   - `Teaching week number - lecture number - lecture name`
   - Example: `S2 Week 2 - L3 - Computer Organization Basics 1`
3. Tutorial lecture videos may use `TL` instead of `L`:
   - Example: `S2 Week 3 - TL1 - Performance`

## Assessment

### Assessment task

| Task | Length | % of module mark |
|---|---:|---:|
| Online exam (centrally scheduled, less than 24 hours) | 3 hours | 100 |

The exam paper contains two parts:

- Part 1 uses MIPS ISA and is based on lectures, exercises, and quizzes.
- Part 2 uses a hypothetical ISA for labs and is based on practical content.
